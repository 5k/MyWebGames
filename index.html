15 Puzzle
Slide numbered tiles into order on a 4×4 grid. One space is empty — can you solve it?
  • Click any tile next to the empty space to slide it in.
  • Use ↑ ↓ ← → arrow keys as an alternative.
  • Arrange tiles 1 – 15 in order, left to right, top to bottom.
  • Hit Shuffle to scramble the board and start a new game.
  • Reset returns the board to the solved state for practice.
  • The timer and move counter start on your first move.

Tic Tac Toe
The timeless 3-in-a-row classic. Play a friend or test yourself against an unbeatable AI.
  • Players take turns: X always goes first.
  • Click any empty square to place your mark.
  • Get 3 in a row — horizontally, vertically, or diagonally — to win.
  • In vs Computer mode, the AI never loses; best you can get is a draw.
  • New Game clears the board; Reset Scores wipes the tally.

Checkers
American Checkers on an 8×8 board. Jump your opponent's pieces and king your men to win.
  • Click a piece to select it — gold dots mark where it can move.
  • Click a destination to move, or click a different piece to re-select.
  • Captures are forced: if you can jump, you must.
  • Chain multiple jumps in one turn when possible.
  • Reach the far side to become a King (♛), which can move in any diagonal direction.
  • Win by leaving your opponent with no legal moves.
  • In vs Computer mode the AI plays Black with minimax + alpha-beta pruning.

Backgammon
Race all 15 checkers around the board and bear them off before your opponent does.
  • Click anywhere on the board (or Roll Dice) to roll at the start of your turn.
  • Click one of your White checkers to select it — gold dots show legal destinations.
  • Click a destination to move. Each move consumes one die value.
  • If you have a checker on the bar, you must re-enter it before moving any other piece.
  • Land on a lone opponent checker to hit it — it goes to the bar.
  • Two or more checkers on a point form a block — opponents can't land there.
  • Rolling doubles gives 4 moves of that value instead of 2.
  • Once all your checkers reach points 1–6, you can start bearing off (removing them from the board).
  • First player to bear off all 15 checkers wins.
